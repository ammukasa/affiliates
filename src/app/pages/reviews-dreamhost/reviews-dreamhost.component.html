Our view on DreamHost:

Affordable:Possibly our favorite thing about DreamHost is that it is the most affordable web hosting service when choosing a month-to-month plan. The monthly billing option means that you can cancel at any time with no additional fees incurred. And the monthly plan doesn’t skimp on the features—it provides the same ones as the annual plans do. The option for monthly billing is ideal for those who either cannot front the kind of money for an annual plan or those who aren’t sure how long they will need a functioning website.
Uptime:Uptime plays a very crucial role in the web hosting experience. You don’t ever want your site to go out, but you want the recovery time to be quick if it does. If people aren’t able to find your site due to it going down, that is bad. Many tests have been run to check the stability of DreamHost, and those tests have all returned with results that show DreamHost is a stable and reliable service that rarely went down during the test periods. There was maybe one time in thousands of different tests that it went down for.
DreamHost is so confident about its uptime that it has a 100% uptime guarantee. DreamHost is the only shared hosting service that offers that high of a guarantee. And if you find that your site is ever down, DreamHost will give you a refund for the amount of time that your site wasn’t up. Now that’s a guarantee we can get behind.
Money-Back Guarantee:Speaking of guarantees, DreamHost has another great one to share with its users. This service comes with a 97-day money-back guarantee, which is something that nearly no other web hosting service can say they offer. Your 97-day trial is completely risk-free, and if you aren’t happy with the service, you get all of your money back. And, as mentioned above, if you aren’t happy with the uptime, you can get compensated for that as well.
However, please note that this money-back guarantee only applies to shared hosting plans and those who purchased their hosting with a credit card or Google Checkout.
Security:Though DreamHost doesn’t offer anti-malware services on its email, there are a lot of things it does right when it comes to security. One of those things is that the control panel makes it easy for you to access and edit the .htaccess file, which is where you control who has access to the site and set up passwords. This is something you want to have easy access to.
In addition, DreamHost offers the ability to block spiders for those parts of your website that you don’t need or want to have indexed by search engines.
And what we really love is that DreamHost offers free SSL certificates, which provides an additional (and, in our opinion, necessary) layer of security for those selling products online or those who are collecting any sort of personal information from clients or customers.
If that’s not enough, there’s more. DreamHost offers SSH and SFTP access, and it also supports HTTP/2, PHP 7, and has its own firewall.
Domain Names: With the purchase of an annual plan (or a longer one), you get a free domain name registration. This makes it easy to do it all in one place. However, do note that the free domain is only for the first year. Each year after that, a .com domain costs $15.95 per year.
You can choose from the most popular domain names out there: .com, .net., .blog, .shop, .org, .info, and more.
Easy to Use: The dashboard of DreamHost is a favorite amongst many. It is modern, clean, and the design was made to be user-friendly.

How DreamHost Pricing Works
DreamHost allows you to choose between monthly and yearly payment options for each of its hosting plans. The Shared and VPS hosting plans also come with a three-year payment option. For all DreamHost hosting plans, if you make the commitment to a yearly or three-year payment option, you’ll save anywhere from 8% to 48% compared to the corresponding monthly option.
For example, you could get the Shared Starter plan for $4.95/month paid monthly, $3.95/month paid in advance for the year, or $2.59/month paid in advance for three years. In this instance, you save 20% by moving from the monthly price to the yearly price, and 48% by moving from the monthly price to the three-year price.
